Philosophy


How I approach learning, building, and growing as a software engineer.



Kaizen: Continuous Improvement


Kaizen is a Japanese philosophy meaning "change for better" or continuous improvement. It's the idea that small, incremental changes compound into remarkable results over time. I don't aim to be perfect. I aim to be 1% better than yesterday.


This philosophy has shaped my journey as a developer. When I started learning to code in 2023, I could barely write a for loop. But I showed up every day, debugged every error, and pushed through every challenge. That consistency took me from beginner to founding and developing Aqua Vitae and building production applications.


What drives me is simple: I love learning and building. Every problem I solve, every project I ship teaches me something valuable. Continuous learning isn't just a buzzword for me. It's how I operate.


What Kaizen means to me:

  • Always learning, always growing
  • Embracing challenges without fear
  • Progress over perfection
  • Small steps, big results


Beyond Code


I apply this same mindset to everything I do. Whether it's rock climbing, strength training, or entrepreneurship, progress comes from showing up consistently and focusing on incremental growth. It's not about being invincible. It's about being unstoppable.


Every project I build, every bug I fix, every line of code I write is part of a larger journey. The goal isn't perfection. The goal is progress.
